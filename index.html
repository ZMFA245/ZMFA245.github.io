European stamps

So far, most of my collectable stamps have been gathered across Europe.

England!

In the England, more specifically London there are 4 major locations where the Lego stamps are avalibale at Lego shops.

These include: London Statford Westfield, London White City Westfield, London's Battersea Powerstation and in my opinion the most iconic of the London branches. London Leicester square.

I am hoping to get the Battersea Powerstation stamp some time in the future.

Denmark!

Home to all Legos! Billund is where the original Legohouse is built

France!

Included are some very iconic stamps from France! First the iconic city stamp of Cathedrale Notre-Dame de Paris. A religious and recongnisable for its intricate architecture. These stamps were collected in Bordeaux's lego shop, Disneyland Paris and Westfield 'Forum des Halles'

The Disneyland stamp came at a surprise but my favourite one from French Lego stores is the Paris Promenade Skyline one found at Westfield 'Forum des halles'.

Italy!

Florence is a beautiful city and I would recommend you visit it, even if you only have the opportunity for a daytripday. I appreciate how in Italy Lego spell Florence as Firenze. As well as they spell Rome as Roma, featuring the grand Colosseum.

Germany!

Munich and Berlin, These lego character's outfits are really unqiue from Germany! The lederhosen design looks really cool when drawn onto the Lego Character.

Asian Stamps

Hong Kong!

Just the one stamp collected from Hong Kong. Found in the New Plaza Mall (and i think discovery centre building), shows a absoulete brilliant dipiction of the Hong Kong Skyline.

Japan!

This stamp was collected inside the Shinshabashi Parco department store, this lego stamp pictures a lady infront of a Japanese snack called takoyaki.

My favourite stamp! A lego figurine pictured next to Mt Fuji, plus the ink colour is green which is different to most of the lego stamps! This was collected on a tour via stopping at an outlet outdoor shopping centre which had a lego store! I think the place was called Gotemba outlet shopping.


I hope after reading this blog page you too now feel inspired to go to a Lego shop near you, and fill your Lego Passport with some iconic stamps!
